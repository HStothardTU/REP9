Teesside: From Industrial Legacy to Net Zero Pioneer

Teesside has long been a powerhouse of industry, driving economic growth and innovation. Today, we stand at the forefront of a new chapter, an era that demands both environmental stewardship and economic resilience. At the NZIIC, we are leading the charge, harnessing our collective expertise to transform Teesside's industrial landscape into a beacon of sustainable progress.

Forging Global Connections

Our commitment to a sustainable future knows no borders. We are proud to be global in outlook, forming powerful relationships with partners around the world. These collaborations amplify our impact, enabling us to tackle the challenges of net zero on a global scale.

The Power of Collaboration and Research

We are a hub of innovation, connecting businesses with the tools, expertise, and resources they need to thrive in the era of net zero. Our multidisciplinary research and development centre houses six cutting-edge labs: Hydrogen Lab, Circular Economy Lab, Smart Energy Lab, Carbon Capture, Storage & Utilisation Lab, Digitalisation Lab, and Innovation Lab. These labs are the engines of our transformative efforts, where collaboration and innovation come together to shape the future.

Teesside University hosts groundbreaking hydrogen conference

Teesside University has brought together industry leaders, researchers, and policymakers to explore the future of hydrogen technologies and their role in achieving Net Zero targets.

UK2070 Teesside TaskForce and Net Zero Industry Innovation Centre forge transatlantic dialogue on a Digital Net Zero Centre in the US

The UK2070 Teesside TaskForce has initiated high-level discussions with US partners to explore the establishment of a Digital Net Zero Centre in the United States. This strategic initiative strengthens international collaboration in clean energy, decarbonisation, and digital innovation, reinforcing the UK’s position as a global leader in the Net Zero transition.

New material could capture millions of tonnes of atmospheric carbon

A new material being developed by Teesside University academics could play a major role in reducing carbon in the atmosphere at a fraction of the cost of traditional carbon dioxide removal technologies.
